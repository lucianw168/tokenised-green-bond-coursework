# Report Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before finalising the report. Every item must pass.

## Structure and Coverage

- [ ] Title is clear, specific, and under 15 words
- [ ] Introduction defines the asset, the approach, and the main argument (120–150 words)
- [ ] Section 1 (Asset) establishes it as a bond FIRST, then green bond, then token
- [ ] Section 2 (Rationale) covers at least 4 tokenisation benefits with specifics
- [ ] Section 2 includes the caveat: tokenisation does not automatically create liquidity
- [ ] Section 3 (Token Design) explains why FT (not NFT) from economic properties
- [ ] Section 3 covers rights, restrictions, and lifecycle in sub-sections
- [ ] Section 3 references GreenBondToken, GitHub link, and testnet CA
- [ ] Section 4 (Market Access) covers the full lifecycle: onboarding → issuance → servicing → secondary
- [ ] Section 4 does NOT overstate liquidity
- [ ] Section 5 (Risk) has at least 5 specific risk categories, each with concrete analysis
- [ ] Section 5 covers: legal, interoperability, liquidity fragmentation, compliance control, smart contract risk
- [ ] Section 6 (DeFi/Blockchain) is restrained — "programmable infrastructure" framing
- [ ] Conclusion gives a clear judgment in 100–120 words without repeating earlier content

## Keyword Coverage

- [ ] "fixed-income security" appears in Section 1
- [ ] "coupon" and "principal repayment" appear in Section 1
- [ ] "use of proceeds" appears in Section 1
- [ ] "investor onboarding" or "transfer administration" appears in Section 2
- [ ] "whitelist" and "restricted transfer" appear in Section 3
- [ ] "primary issuance" and "secondary transfer" appear in Section 4
- [ ] "professional / qualified investors" appears in Section 4
- [ ] "legal coherence" or "legal recognition" appears in Section 5
- [ ] "settlement finality" or "interoperability" appears in Section 5
- [ ] "liquidity fragmentation" appears in Section 5

## Figures and Evidence

- [ ] At least 1 figure included (lifecycle diagram or rights-and-controls diagram)
- [ ] Figures are referenced in-text, not just appended
- [ ] Implementation note appears in Section 3 with CA, explorer link, GitHub link
- [ ] Screenshots referenced as Figures A1–A8 in appendix or implementation note
- [ ] All CA values match across report, README, and deployment JSON

## Tone and Framing

- [ ] Asset is framed as "compliant digital security", not "crypto token"
- [ ] Token is framed as "on-chain representation of bond claim", not standalone asset
- [ ] Green attribute is treated as use-of-proceeds constraint, not marketing label
- [ ] Liquidity claims are qualified and cautious
- [ ] Risk section is specific, not generic hand-waving
- [ ] DeFi section is supportive, not evangelical

## Prohibited Framings (MUST NOT appear)

- [ ] Does NOT call the token "ESG coin", "green crypto", or "environmental NFT"
- [ ] Does NOT claim "24/7 trading therefore liquid"
- [ ] Does NOT claim "anyone can trade freely"
- [ ] Does NOT claim "DeFi solves liquidity"
- [ ] Does NOT list "regulation is unclear" as a standalone risk without specifics
- [ ] Does NOT describe risks as only "hacking" and "volatility"

## References

- [ ] At least 3 substantive references cited
- [ ] Coursework brief cited for scope
- [ ] At least 1 regulatory source (HKMA, SFC, BIS, IOSCO)
- [ ] At least 1 tokenisation industry source (Tokeny, DTCC/BCG, etc.)
- [ ] Reference format is consistent throughout

## Word Count

- [ ] Total word count ≤ 2,400 (excluding references and appendix)
- [ ] No section exceeds its budget by more than 50 words
- [ ] Risk section has at least 400 words
